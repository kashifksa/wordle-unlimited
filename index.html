How to Play: Rules and Mechanics

The goal is simple: Guess the hidden five letter word in six attempts. After every guess, the tiles change color to show how close you are to the answer:

Green: The letter is correct and in the perfect spot.

Yellow: The letter is in the word but in a different position.

Gray: The letter is not part of today’s secret word.

Best Strategies for Consistent Wins

Winning at Wordle requires more than just guessing. Use these proven methods to find the hidden word in fewer attempts.

Choosing Your Starting Word

  • Begin with words that contain many vowels like ARISE or ADIEU.
  • Focus on common consonants such as S, T, R, L, and N.
  • Avoid using double letters in your first guess to test more unique characters.

The Second Guess Strategy

  • Use your second attempt to eliminate as many remaining letters as possible.
  • If your first word gave no hits, choose an entirely different set of letters.
  • Do not repeat gray letters that have already been ruled out.

Understanding Wordle Colors and Logic

The feedback system is simple but powerful. Knowing how to read the tiles is the key to solving any puzzle.

What the Tile Colors Mean

  • Green tiles mean the letter is correct and it is in the right position.
  • Yellow tiles mean the letter exists in the word but it is in a different spot.
  • Gray tiles mean the letter is not part of the hidden word at all.

Advanced Tips for Hard Mode Players

  • Always use the hints provided by green and yellow tiles in your next guess.
  • Think about common word endings like ING or ED when you have a few letters.
  • Practice regularly in unlimited mode to learn new five letter words and patterns.

Expert Wordle Strategies

Top Starting Words

The first guess is the most critical. Math experts and linguists suggest using words with many vowels and common consonants.

  • ADIEU
  • ARISE
  • STARE
  • AUDIO

The Vowel Hunter

If your first word didn't reveal vowels, use your second turn to test the remaining ones (O, U, and sometimes Y).

Pattern Recognition

Watch out for common word endings like ING, ED, or ER. They can help you narrow down the target word quickly.

Double Letter Trick

Don't forget that target words can contain double letters (like E or O). If you're stuck, try repeating a vowel or common consonant.

Who Made the Original Wordle?

+

The original Wordle game was created by Welsh software engineer Josh Wardle in 2021. He made the game as a fun project for his partner, who loved word puzzles. Later Wordle became a worldwide viral game and was bought by The New York Times (NYT) in 2022.
