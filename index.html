How to Play: Rules and Mechanics

The objective is straightforward: identify the hidden five letter English word within six guesses. Every guess must be a valid word. After each submission, the tiles change color to reveal how accurate your guess was:

Green: The letter is correct and sits exactly where it belongs.

Yellow: The letter appears in the target word but occupies a different position.

Gray: The letter is not present in the hidden word at all.

Reading this feedback accurately and adjusting each subsequent guess is the entire game. It sounds simple. Mastering it is another matter entirely.

Master the Game: Proven Wordle Strategies

Expert Wordle Strategies

Top Starting Words

Linguists and puzzle mathematicians broadly agree on a shortlist of optimal openers. These words maximize letter coverage across all five positions in a single guess.

  • ADIEU
  • ARISE
  • STARE
  • AUDIO

The Vowel Hunter

If your opening word fails to reveal any vowels, use your second guess to target the ones you missed. English five-letter words almost always contain at least two vowels. Once you know which vowels are present, your candidate pool shrinks dramatically. The letters O, U, and the semi-vowel Y are worth hunting if A, E, and I came back gray.

Pattern Recognition

Experienced players develop a feel for common English word structures. Watching for recurring endings like ING, ED, ER, and EST is not cheating. It is applied linguistics. Spotting these patterns from partial clues often lets you identify the target word one or two guesses earlier than random elimination would allow.

The Double Letter Trap

One of the most common mistakes is assuming every letter in the target word appears only once. English frequently produces five letter words with repeated letters, especially doubled vowels like E and O, and common consonants like L and S. If you are stuck with three or four confirmed letters and cannot form a word, try repeating one of them.

+

Josh Wardle, a Welsh software engineer, built Wordle in 2021 as a personal gift for his partner. The game went viral within months and was acquired by The New York Times in early 2022.

The backend draws from a curated pool of valid English five-letter words and selects them in a way that ensures variety across consecutive games. The goal is to keep every session genuinely fresh rather than cycling through the same short list.
